About our ffconf workshop

  • Full Day Event

    I don't know about you, but being a web developer at time's can be Daunting. There is so much to learn and everybody else seems to know everything else about it. No sooner have you mastered a new skill, you are told, that's yesterday's news "have you tried pickanoun.js it's so much better" (despite that persons complete knowledge of pickanoun.js being an overheard conversation at a meetup).

  • Live Code and Demos

    That's why we built this workshop, to remove the hype and bravado surrounding todays Frontend Web Development landscape and just leave you with a practical understanding of many popular web development techniques. The workshop will be led by Martin Beeby and Martin Kearn who both work for Microsoft and travel the country explaining web technologies to developers.

  • See what’s new

    We will mix presentations with hands on labs in this full day workshop aimed at anyone that wants to get up close and personal with technologies that, they may have heard about, but just haven't found the time to explore.

  • Labs

    We will mix presentations with hands on labs in this full day workshop aimed at anyone that wants to get up close and personal with technologies that, they may have heard about, but just haven't found the time to explore.

What you’ll learn

Web Interoperability with Microsoft Edge, tuning and optimising your site for real web users.

Building and architecting apps with frameworks like Angular.

Adopting tools to make web development workflows easier.

Discover the new features in the ES6/2015 JavaScript Specification and how to use them.

Speakers

Martin Kearn

Twitter, Blog

Martin Kearn is a Technical Evangelist at Microsoft UK. If he's not helping partners build apps or web sites you'll find him traveling the country speaking at events about web development (ASP.net, Edge, Azure) and Office.

He has a passion (teetering on the edge of obsession) for web performance and optimisation, and enjoys nothing more than squeezing an extra 10ms from a page load.

Martin Beeby

Twitter, Blog

Martin Beeby works for Microsoft where he talks to developers about HTML5, Windows 10 and the web. Martin has been developing since he was 16 and over the past 17 years has worked on projects with many Major brands

Martin has written articles for, and been featured in, .NET Magazine, ZDNet, the Microsoft Developer Network, Ubelly.com and Windows Magazine.
